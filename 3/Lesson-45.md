# Lesson 45: The Power of the Press

## 课文内容 (Text)

In democratic countries any efforts to restrict the freedom of the press are rightly condemned. However, this freedom can easily be abused. Stories about people often attract far more public attention than political events.

Though we may enjoy reading about the lives of others, it is extremely doubtful whether we would equally enjoy reading about ourselves. Acting on the contention that facts are sacred, reporters can cause untold suffering to individuals by publishing details about their private lives.

Newspapers exert such tremendous influence that they can not only bring about major changes to the lives of ordinary people but can even overthrow a government. The story of a poor family that acquired fame and fortune overnight, dramatically illustrates the power of the press. The family lived in Aberdeen, a small town of 23, 000 inhabitants in South Dakota.

As the parents had five children, life was a perpetual struggle against poverty. They were expecting their sixth child and were faced with even more pressing economic problems. If they had only had one more child, the fact would have passed unnoticed.

They would have continued to struggle against economic odds and would have lived in obscurity. But they suddenly became the parents of quintuplets, four girls and a boy, an event which radically changed their lives. The day after the birth of the five children, an aeroplane arrived in Aberdeen bringing sixty reporters and photographers.

The rise to fame was swift. Television cameras and newspapers carried the news to everyone in the country. Newspapers and magazines offered the family huge sums for the exclusive rights to publish stories and photographs.

Gifts poured in not only from unknown people, but from baby food and soap manufacturers who wished to advertise their products. The old farmhouse the family lived in was to be replaced by a new $500, 000 home.

Reporters kept pressing for interviews so lawyers had to be employed to act as spokesmen for the family at press conferences. While the five babies were still quietly sleeping in oxygen tents in a hospital nursery, their parents were paying the price for fame.

It would never again be possible for them to lead normal lives. They had become the victims of commercialization, for their names had acquired a market value. Instead of being five new family members, these children had immediately become a commodity.

## 语法要点解析 (Grammar & Key Structures)

### 1. 现在分词作宾语补足语 (Present Participle as Object Complement)
* **经典原句**：*We saw the newspapers printing the news.*
* **核心解析**：
printing the news 是现在分词短语，作宾语 newspapers 的补足语，表示动作正在发生。

## 写作仿写与思辨口语 (Writing Practice & Critical Thinking)

### 1. ✍️ 提分句型仿写 (Pattern Drill)
* **句型名称**：Lesson 45 Writing Structure
* **句型公式**：`Subject + would have done + if + Subject + had + past participle`
* **仿写练习 1**：如果你当时提前通知我，我就能按时在机场接你了。 (If you had informed me in advance, I would have picked you up at the airport on time.)
* **仿写练习 2**：如果团队没有在关键时刻妥协，该项目本可以获得更大的成功。 (The project would have achieved greater success if the team had not compromised at the critical moment.)

### 2. 🔍 近义词/易混词辨析 (Word Discrimination)
* **对比辨析**：**acquire vs. obtain**
* **使用差异解析**：
`obtain` 指通过努力或请求“得到、获得”具体的东西（如证书、许可、信息）。而 `acquire` 侧重于“**逐渐习得，学到**”技能、知识，或“收购、购置”资产，多指一个渐进的过程。
* **地道表达搭配**：
* acquire skills (习得技能)
* obtain permission (获得许可)
* language acquisition (语言习得)
