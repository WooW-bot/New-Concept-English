# Lesson 60: Too Early and Too Late

## 课文内容 (Text)

Punctuality is a necessary habit in all public affairs in civilized society. Without it, nothing could ever be brought to a conclusion; everything would be in a state of chaos. Only in a sparsely-populated rural community is it possible to disregard it.

In ordinary living, there can be some tolerance of unpunctuality. The intellectual, who is working on some abstruse problem, has everything coordinated and organized for the matter in hand. He is therefore forgiven if late for a dinner party.

But people are often reproached for unpunctuality when their only fault is cutting things fine. It is hard for energetic, quick-minded people to waste time, so they are often tempted to finish a job before setting out to keep an appointment.

If no accidents occur on the way, like punctured tyres, diversions of traffic, sudden descent of fog, they will be on time. They are often more industrious, useful citizens than those who are never late. The over-punctual can be as much a trial to others as the unpunctual.

The guest who arrives half an hour too soon is the greatest nuisance. Some friends of my family had this irritating habit. The only thing to do was ask them to come half an hour later than the other guests.

Then they arrived just when we wanted them. If you are catching a train, it is always better to be comfortably early than even a fraction of a minute too late. Although being early may mean wasting a little time, this will be less than if you miss the train and have to wait an hour or more for the next one; and you avoid the frustration of arriving at the very moment when the train is drawing out of the station and being unable to get on it.

An even harder situation is to be on the platform in good time for a train and still to see it go off without you. Such an experience befell a certain young girl the first time she was travelling alone. She entered the station twenty minutes before the train was due, since her parents had impressed upon her that it would be unforgivable to miss it and cause the friends with whom she was going to stay to make two journeys to meet her.

She gave her luggage to a porter and showed him her ticket. To her horror he said that she was two hours too soon. She felt in her handbag for the piece of paper on which her father had written down all the details of the journey and gave it to the porter.

He agreed that a train did come into the station at the time on the paper and that it did stop, but only to take on mail, not passengers. The girl asked to see a timetable, feeling sure that her father could not have made such a mistake. The porter went to fetch one and arrived back with the station master, who produced it with a flourish and pointed out a microscopic 'o' beside the time of the arrival of the train at his station; this little 'o' indicated that the train only stopped for mail.

Just as that moment the train came into the station. The girl, tears streaming down her face, begged to be allowed to slip into the guard's van. But the station master was adamant: rules could not be broken.

And she had to watch that train disappear towards her destination while she was left behind.

## 语法要点解析 (Grammar & Key Structures)

### 1. 动名词作主语 (Gerund as Subject)
* **经典原句**：*Although being early may mean wasting a little time...*
* **核心解析**：
**being early** 和 **wasting a little time** 分别为动名词短语。
* **语法意义**：动名词具有动词的特征（可带宾语或状语）和名词的特征（在句中作主语、宾语等）。此处 `being early` 作从句的主语，`wasting...` 作动词 `mean` 的宾语。

### 2. 时间状语从句中的分词短语作状语 (Participle Clause in Adverbial Clause)
* **经典原句**：*...so they are often tempted to finish a job before setting out to keep an appointment.*
* **核心解析**：
**before setting out...** 中，`before` 是介词（也可以看作连词省略主语），后面接动名词/现在分词短语，作时间状语。这相当于：`before they set out...`。

### 3. 定语从句中的介词前置 (Preposition + Relative Pronoun)
* **经典原句**：*...and cause the friends with whom she was going to stay to make two journeys...*
* **核心解析**：
**with whom she was going to stay** 是修饰 `friends` 的定语从句。
* **结构解析**：动词短语 `stay with`（和...住在一起）中的介词 `with` 提前，置于指人的关系代词 `whom` 之前。这是标准的正式书面语结构，口语中通常说成：`the friends whom/that she was going to stay with`。

## 写作仿写与思辨口语 (Writing Practice & Critical Thinking)

### 1. ✍️ 提分句型仿写 (Pattern Drill)
* **句型名称**：It is always better to... than... (做……总比……好)
* **句型公式**：`It is always better to + [动词原形A] + than (to) + [动词原形B]`
* **仿写练习 1**：早做准备总比面临突发事件时手忙脚乱要好。 (It is always better to prepare early than to panic in the face of unexpected events.)
* **仿写练习 2**：买一个质量好的包总比频繁更换便宜的包要划算。 (It is always better to buy a high-quality bag than to replace cheap ones frequently.)

### 2. 🔍 近义词/易混词辨析 (Word Discrimination)
* **对比辨析**：**adamant vs. stubborn**
* **使用差异解析**：
两个词均表示“坚决、不妥协”。`stubborn`（顽固的，倔强的）通常是贬义词，指一个人不讲道理、盲目拒绝改变主意。而 `adamant`（坚决的，固执的）多为中性或褒义词，指基于某种信念、规则或坚定事实而“**绝不退让、无比坚定**”。
* **地道表达搭配**：
* be adamant that... (坚称/坚决认为...)
* stubborn stain (顽固污渍)
* stubborn refusal (死硬的拒绝)

### 3. 💬 课后思辨讨论题 (Speaking & Writing Prompts)
* **Question 1**: In your opinion, are strict rules (like those in railway timetables) necessary, or should officials have the flexibility to make exceptions?
* **Question 2**: How do you handle friends or business partners who are consistently late or excessively early?
* **高分表达提示 (Hint Words)**: *unyielding (不屈服的), strictly adhere to (严格遵守), rule-bound (拘泥于规则的), human touch (人情味), social etiquette (社交礼仪)*
