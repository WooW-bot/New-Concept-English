# Lesson 36: A Chance in a Million

## 课文内容 (Text)

We are less credulous than we used to be. In the nineteenth century, a novelist would bring his story to a conclusion by presenting his readers with a series of coincidences--most of them wildly improbable.

Readers happily accepted the fact that an obscure maidservant was really the hero's mother. A long-lost brother, who was presumed dead, was really alive all the time and wickedly plotting to bring about the hero's downfall. And so on.

Modern readers would find such naive solutions totally unacceptable. Yet, in real life, circumstances do sometimes conspire to bring about coincidences which anyone but a nineteenth century novelist would find incredible. When I was a boy, my grandfather told me how a German taxi driver, Franz Bussman, found a brother who was thought to have been killed twenty years before.

While on a walking tour with his wife, he stopped to talk to a workman. After they had gone on, Mrs. Bussman commented on the workman's close resemblance to her husband and even suggested that he might be his brother.

Franz poured scorn on the idea, pointing out that his brother had been killed in action during the war. Though Mrs. Bussman was fully acquainted with this story, she thought that there was a chance in a million that she might be right.

A few days later, she sent a boy to the workman to ask him if his name was Hans Bussman. Needless to say, the man's name was Hans Bussman and he really was Franz's long-lost brother. When the brothers were reunited, Hans explained how it was that he was still alive.

After having been wounded towards the end of the war, he had been sent to hospital and was separated from his unit. The hospital had been bombed and Hans had made his way back into Western Germany on foot.

Meanwhile, his unit was lost and all records of him had been destroyed. Hans returned to his family home, but the house had been bombed and no one in the neighbourhood knew what had become of the inhabitants.

Assuming that his family had been killed during an air raid, Hans settled down in a village fifty miles away where he had remained ever since.

### 1. 同位语从句 (Appositive Clause)
* **经典原句**：*The chance that he would win the lottery was small.*
* **核心解析**：
that he would win... 是同位语从句，解释说明名词 chance 的具体内容。that 在同位语从句中只起连接作用，不作任何成分。

## 写作仿写与思辨口语 (Writing Practice & Critical Thinking)

### 1. ✍️ 提分句型仿写 (Pattern Drill)
* **句型名称**：Lesson 36 Writing Structure
* **句型公式**：`Subject + would have done + if + Subject + had + past participle`
* **仿写练习 1**：如果你当时提前通知我，我就能按时在机场接你了。 (If you had informed me in advance, I would have picked you up at the airport on time.)
* **仿写练习 2**：如果团队没有在关键时刻妥协，该项目本可以获得更大的成功。 (The project would have achieved greater success if the team had not compromised at the critical moment.)

### 2. 🔍 近义词/易混词辨析 (Word Discrimination)
* **对比辨析**：**acquire vs. obtain**
* **使用差异解析**：
`obtain` 指通过努力或请求“得到、获得”具体的东西（如证书、许可、信息）。而 `acquire` 侧重于“**逐渐习得，学到**”技能、知识，或“收购、购置”资产，多指一个渐进的过程。
* **地道表达搭配**：
* acquire skills (习得技能)
* obtain permission (获得许可)
* language acquisition (语言习得)
