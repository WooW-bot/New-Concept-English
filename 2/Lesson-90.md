# Lesson 90: What's for Supper

## 课文内容 (Text)

Fish and chips has always been a favourite dish in Britain, but as the oceans have been overfished, fish has become more and more expensive.
炸鱼加炸薯条在英国一直是一道受人喜爱的菜肴，但由于海洋过度捕捞，鱼变得越来越贵了。
So it comes as a surprise to learn that giant fish are terrifying the divers on North Sea oil rigs.
因此，得知北海石油钻井平台上的潜水员正受到巨鱼的惊吓，这令人感到十分惊讶。

Oil rigs have to be repaired frequently and divers, who often have to work in darkness a hundred feet under water, have been frightened out of their wits by giant fish bumping into them as they work.
石油钻井平台需要经常维修，而经常不得不在水下百英尺黑暗中工作的潜水员们，在工作时常被撞向他们的巨鱼吓得魂不附体。
Now they have had special cages made to protect them from these monsters.
现在，他们已经特制了防护笼，以保护自己免受这些怪物的伤害。

The fish are not sharks or killer whales, but favourite eating varieties like cod and skate which grow to unnatural sizes, sometimes as much as twelve feet in length.
这些鱼并非鲨鱼或虎鲸，而是像鳕鱼和鳐鱼这样人们爱吃的品种，它们长到了不正常的尺寸，有时身长竟达12英尺。
Three factors have caused these fish to grow so large: the warm water round the hot oil pipes under the sea; the plentiful supply of food thrown overboard by the crews on the rigs; and the total absence of fishing boats around the oil rigs.
三个因素导致这些鱼长得如此巨大：海底热油管周围温暖的水域；钻井平台工作人员抛入海中的充足食物；以及石油钻井平台周围完全没有渔船。
As a result, the fish just eat and eat and grow and grow in the lovely warm water.
结果，这些鱼就在那舒适温暖的水中不停地吃，不停地长。
Who eats who?
究竟是谁吃谁呢？

## 语法要点解析 (Grammar & Key Structures)

### 1. 现在完成被动语态 (Present Perfect Passive)
* **经典原句**：*...as the oceans have been overfished, fish has become more and more expensive.*
* **核心解析**：
**have been overfished** 表示从过去持续到现在的一个动作，且强调动作的结果对现在的影响。
* **结构解析**：`have/has + been + 过去分词`。此处强调“过度捕捞”这一行为导致了目前“鱼价上涨”的现状。

### 2. 定语从句中的介词 + 关系代词 (Preposition + Relative Pronoun)
* **经典原句**：*...divers, who often have to work in darkness a hundred feet under water, have been frightened out of their wits by giant fish bumping into them as they work.*
* **核心解析**：
本句中包含多个从句。`who often have to work...` 是修饰 `divers` 的非限制性定语从句。
* **结构解析**：在定语从句中，如果关系代词在从句中作介词的宾语，介词通常可以提前。例如：*The rigs on which they work are dangerous.*（他们工作的钻井平台很危险）。

### 3. 使役动词用法 (Have something done)
* **经典原句**：*Now they have had special cages made to protect them from these monsters.*
* **核心解析**：
**have had... made** 表示“请人做某事”或“让某事被做”。
* **结构解析**：`have + 宾语 + 过去分词`。这里的 `have` 是使役动词，表示主语（潜水员）并不是自己制造笼子，而是雇佣或安排他人完成了这项工作。

## 写作仿写与思辨口语 (Writing Practice & Critical Thinking)

### 1. ✍️ 提分句型仿写 (Pattern Drill)
* **句型名称**：It comes as a surprise to learn that... (得知……令人感到惊讶)
* **句型公式**：`It comes as a surprise to [do sth] that + [从句]`
* **仿写练习 1**：得知这种罕见的植物竟然能在沙漠中生长，真是令人惊讶。(It comes as a surprise to learn that this rare plant can actually grow in the desert.)
* **仿写练习 2**：得知他竟然放弃了高薪工作去支教，这令人感到惊讶。(It comes as a surprise to learn that he gave up his high-paying job to teach in rural areas.)

### 2. 🔍 近义词/易混词辨析 (Word Discrimination)
* **对比辨析**：**frighten vs. scare**
* **使用差异解析**：
`frighten` 语气较强，通常指突然的、强烈的惊吓，使人感到不安或恐惧；`scare` 语气相对较轻，常指一般的害怕或胆怯。在表达“被吓坏了”时，常用 `be frightened out of one's wits`（固定搭配，意为“吓得魂飞魄散”），而 `scare` 常用于 `scare away`（吓跑）。
* **地道表达搭配**：
* frighten the life out of someone (把某人吓个半死)
* scare the wits out of someone (同上)

### 3. 💬 课后思辨讨论题 (Speaking & Writing Prompts)
* **Question 1**: Human activities often have unintended consequences on nature. Can you think of another example where human interference has changed the behavior or size of wild animals?
* **Question 2**: The text ends with "Who eats who?" What does this imply about the relationship between humans and the environment?
* **高分表达提示 (Hint Words)**: *unintended consequences (意想不到的后果), ecological balance (生态平衡), human interference (人类干预), adaptation (适应), sustainability (可持续性)*
