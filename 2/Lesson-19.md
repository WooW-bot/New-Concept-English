# Lesson 19: Sold Out

## 课文内容 (Text)

'The play may begin at any moment,' I said. 'It may have begun already,' Susan answered. I hurried to the ticket office. 'May I have two tickets please?' I asked. 'I'm sorry, we've sold out,' the girl said. 'What a pity!' Susan exclaimed.

Just then, a man hurried to the ticket office. 'Can I return these two tickets?' he asked. 'Certainly,' the girl said. I went back to the ticket office at once. 'Could I have those two tickets please?' I asked. 'Certainly,' the girl said, 'but they're for next Wednesday's performance. Do you still want them?'

'I might as well have them,' I said sadly.

## 语法要点解析 (Grammar & Key Structures)

### 1. 情态动词 `may`, `can`, `could` 表示可能性和请求

*   **经典原句**：
    *   `The play may begin at any moment.`
    *   `May I have two tickets please?`
    *   `Can I return these two tickets?`
    *   `Could I have those two tickets please?`
*   **核心解析**：
    *   **`may`** 表示可能性（可能发生，但不太确定）或提出请求（语气比 `can` 更正式、客气）。
    *   **`can`** 表示能力（能够做某事）或提出请求（语气较直接）。
    *   **`could`** 是 `can` 的过去式，但在请求中常用来表示比 `can` 更委婉、客气的语气，或表示过去的能力。
*   **结构解析**：
    *   可能性：`主语 + may/can/could + 动词原形`
    *   请求：`May/Can/Could + 主语 + 动词原形...?`

### 2. 情态动词 `may` + `have done` 表对过去事情的推测

*   **经典原句**：`It may have begun already,' Susan answered.`
*   **核心解析**：
    **`may have begun`** 表示对过去发生的事情进行不确定的推测（“可能已经... / 或许已经...”）。
*   **结构解析**：情态动词 `may` 加上动词的完成式 `have + 过去分词`，用于表示对过去动作的不确定推测。类似的结构还有 `might have done`（可能性更小），`must have done`（肯定推测），`can't have done`（否定推测）。

### 3. 句型 `I might as well...` 表示“不妨，还是...好”

*   **经典原句**：`'I might as well have them,' I said sadly.`
*   **核心解析**：
    **`I might as well...`** 表示在没有更好选择的情况下，或在某种无奈的情绪下，认为做某事也无妨，或者说“反正也这样了，不如就...”。常带有勉强、无奈或无所谓的情绪。
*   **结构解析**：`主语 + might as well + 动词原形`。这个短语强调在当前情境下，做某事是同样合理或唯一的选择，即使不是最理想的。

## 写作仿写与思辨口语 (Writing Practice & Critical Thinking)

### 1. ✍️ 提分句型仿写 (Pattern Drill)

*   **句型名称**：What a pity! (真可惜！/真遗憾！)
*   **句型公式**：`What a pity!` 或 `What a pity that + [从句]`
*   **仿写练习 1**：真可惜，我们错过了最后一班火车！ (What a pity that we missed the last train!)
*   **仿写练习 2**：真遗憾，你不能来参加我们的派对。 (What a pity you can't come to our party.)

### 2. 🔍 近义词/易混词辨析 (Word Discrimination)

*   **对比辨析**：**may vs. might**
*   **使用差异解析**：
    `may` 和 `might` 都可以表示可能性，但 `might` 通常表示的可能性比 `may` 更小，语气更不确定。在口语中，`may` 也可以用于表示请求（更正式），而 `might` 则不常用于请求，更多用于委婉的建议或批评。
*   **地道表达搭配**：
    *   **may**: May I come in? (我可以进来吗？) / It may rain tomorrow. (明天可能会下雨。)
    *   **might**: It might be true. (那也许是真的，可能性较小。) / You might try calling him. (你或许可以试着给他打电话。)

### 3. 💬 课后思辨讨论题 (Speaking & Writing Prompts)

*   **Question 1**: Have you ever missed an important event (like a concert, a movie, or a flight) because you didn't plan ahead or encountered an unexpected problem? How did you feel?
*   **Question 2**: The narrator in the story bought tickets for the wrong date. What steps do you usually take to ensure you have the correct information when booking tickets or making reservations?
*   **高分表达提示 (Hint Words)**: *regret (后悔), disappointment (失望), plan ahead (提前计划), book in advance (提前预订), double-check (仔细核对), unexpected (意想不到的)*
